Our Commitment

SMPLTSK is committed to ensuring digital accessibility for people with disabilities. We continually improve the user experience for everyone and apply relevant accessibility standards.

Accessibility Standards

We aim to conform to the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1, Level AA standards. These guidelines help make web and mobile content more accessible to people with disabilities, including:

  • Visual impairments
  • Hearing impairments
  • Motor impairments
  • Cognitive disabilities

Accessibility in SMPLTSK App

Visual Features

  • Clear Visual Markers: Our red and green markers use shapes in addition to colors for colorblind users
  • Text Alternatives: All images can have text descriptions added to task markers
  • Adjustable Text Size: Respects system font size preferences
  • Simple Interface: Clean, uncluttered design reduces cognitive load

Motor Accessibility

  • Large Touch Areas: All buttons exceed 44x44 pixel minimum touch target
  • Gesture Alternatives: All actions available through taps, no complex gestures required
  • Confirmation Dialogs: Prevents accidental deletions with clear confirmation prompts

Cognitive Accessibility

  • Visual-First Design: Reduces text requirements, ideal for users with dyslexia or reading difficulties
  • Simple Workflows: Three-step process (Capture, Mark, Complete) is easy to remember
  • Consistent Navigation: Predictable interface patterns throughout the app
  • Error Prevention: Clear warnings before destructive actions

Known Limitations

While we strive for full accessibility, some areas are still being improved:

  • Complex image annotations may require visual confirmation
  • Some older Android devices may have limited screen reader functionality
  • Video content does not yet have captions (planned for future release)

Ongoing Improvements

We are actively working on:

  • Adding voice commands for hands-free task creation
  • Implementing dark mode for reduced eye strain
  • Enhancing audio feedback for task completion
  • Adding haptic feedback options
  • Improving screen reader descriptions for complex visual elements

Testing & Validation

Our accessibility efforts include:

  • Regular testing with screen readers and assistive technologies
  • User testing with people who have disabilities
  • Automated accessibility scanning on each app update
  • Manual keyboard navigation testing
  • Color contrast validation tools

Third-Party Content

While we ensure our app is accessible, we cannot guarantee the accessibility of:

  • Content shared from SMPLTSK to external apps
  • Third-party app stores where SMPLTSK is downloaded
  • External websites linked from our support pages

Compliance Status

We believe SMPLTSK is partially conformant with WCAG 2.1 Level AA. Partially conformant means that some parts of the content do not fully conform to the accessibility standard. We are actively working toward full conformance.

Legal Requirements

We aim to meet or exceed requirements under:

  • Australian Disability Discrimination Act 1992 (DDA)
  • Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1
  • Section 508 of the Rehabilitation Act (US users)
  • European Accessibility Act (EU users)

Updates to This Statement

We update this accessibility statement as we improve features and address issues. Check back periodically for updates on our accessibility efforts.

Building for Everyone

At SMPLTSK, we believe everyone deserves tools that work for them. Our visual approach to task management is designed to be intuitive for all users, regardless of their abilities. We're committed to continuous improvement and welcome your feedback.
